Tutorial

What is \code?

How to pronounce; Reason to exist

\code (pronounced /ˈbækˌslæʃ,kəʊd/) is a Google Chrome extension that adds macros and mnemonics to common form fields on the Internet. With \code you won't need to remember obscure UNICODE hexadecimal numbers or HTML entity codes, you only need to remember there are \codes and \\macros.

UNICODE; \codes and \\macros

\codes are short mnemonics for UNICODE symbols\---a collection of numbers defined to represent glpyhs in many of the world\aposs languages. A mnemonic is a brief word used to remember something, in \code there are many mnemonics for various UNICODE characters.

Examples of \code before replacing or expansion
  • A \subset B \notequals C
  • \\lod
  • \odqFind a job you love and you\aposll never work a day in your life.\cdq\----Confucius
  • \\creep
  • \blackstar \rarr \star
  • a\^2 + b\^2 = c\^2
  • \\lincoln

Macros and Mnemonics

As you can see, there are two different kinds of BScodes: \\macros and \mnemonics and they are replaced by with their symbols or text whenever you pause during typing. Macros can be very short chunks of text or relatively long form letters to be reused, as with Abraham Lincoln's Gettysburg Address.

\\enable and \\disable

Notice the red-border on the right side of the field? This is your cue to know that \code is supported in that field. You can use these anywhere on the Internet that uses supported fields. If you wish to disable \code in a field temporarilly, enter in \\disable, and the right border will change to a dotted border. To turn \code back on, enter in \\enable. Try it for yourself in the field above and watch the right border.
